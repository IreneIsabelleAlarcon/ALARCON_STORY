THE SHOE THAT FLEW

When Ollie does a GIANT kick one sunny day, It's not just the ball that goes flying!

"Tie up your shoe, Ollie", Mum called.
"Watch out you don't trip!" Ollie's sister called.
But Ollie didn't hear.

"Tie up your shoe, Ollie", Mum called.
"Watch out you don't trip!" Ollie's sister called.
But Ollie didn't hear.

Ollie was playing ball; he was having fun.
The ball was doing evrything he wanted. He ran with the ball.
he bounced the ball. He tapped the ball with his knee.
Ollie was in control.

"Watch me!" He laughed and bounced the ball,
before he gave it one mighty kick.
The mighty kick went straight to the goal post Ollie had marked on the garden wall...

Mum shouted out, "What a shoe!"

Ollie's sister yelled even louder, "What a Kick!"

Grandma was a bit squeakier...
What a flying shoe!

And they all stopped to watch Ollie's ball AND his shoe go flying,
as high as could be.

"Oooh...!"
They all watched.
"My shoe's flying!" called Ollie.
And so it was Ollie's shoe and the ball were flying high could be,
and before they knew it - they has both flown way over the garden wall...

Shoe and ball were gone! They all stopped listen.
Joey, the neighbour's dog, was barking, but then he stopped...
"He's got the ball..." Ollie was sure.

Ollie and his sister scrabbled about to find things to stand on.
Grandma pointed at a pile of cracked old flower pots.
Ollie climbed up onto the woblly pile. He could just peek over the wall.

Joey the dog had the ball! He was jumping around and having fun.
Joey's tail was wagging like crazy , and the ball was bouncing.
That ball was bouncing around and up and down!

"How do i get my ball back?" Ollie wondered out loud.
"Can I have my ball back?"
Ollie call out to the dog.
"And my shoe! it flew, too!"

"Woof woof!"
Joey the dog was far too busy playing with the ball, and the shoe...

" Can I have my ball back?" Ollie tried again.
Two black and white birds tweeted up in a tree - it sounded like

Maybe, Maybe!

" Can I have my ball back?" Ollie shouted a little louder. "And my shoe, too?"
A cat walked along the wall...

Meow meow, not now...

Ollie's Grandma had heard Ollie shouting. she came nearer to see.
"Ahh!" she laughed. " Did that ball go flying over the wall with your shoes?"
Ollie laughed."Flying shoes! Who knew shoes could fly?"

Grandma and Ollie tried another shout.
"Can we have our ball back, and our flying shoe?"
The dog stopped barking, and a boy's voice shouted,
"Take cover! Watch out for one flying shoes coming over to you..!"

"Oooh..."
Grandma and Ollie took cover behind a tree, and the flying shoe came whizzing over the wall.
The flying shoes landed in the branch of the tree, and those two birds went squawking off in
a flurry of black and white feathers!
Grandma was tall enough - well, she needed a twig - but Ollie got his flying shoe back.

"Tie up your shoe!" Grandma shouted, a bit loud.
Ollie's sister laughed. "Nice and tight!"
Ollie put on his flying shoe and climbed up onto the flower-pot pile.
"Thanks foe the flying shoe. Can I have my ball, too?" Ollie called.
"No, you can't!" the boy over the wall shouted back.

Ollie looked at Grandma.
Grandma looked at Mum.
Mum looked at Ollie...
Nobody said anthing at all. Then Ollie called,"Why not?"
The boy over the wall called back...
"Come and get it, and we can play!"

THE END
